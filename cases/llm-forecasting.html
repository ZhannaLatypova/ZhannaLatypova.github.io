Integration of LLM and Time Series Analysis in Demand Forecasting for E-Commerce

By integrating textual insights from customer reviews and trend data with historical sales patterns, the project demonstrates a data-driven approach to improving inventory management, pricing strategies, and marketing efficiency.

Client
Online seller Marketplace Wildberries

The company ranks among the top five sellers of UGG footwear, holding a leading market position thanks to high online sales volume and an effective distribution strategy.

Business Problem
Optimize inventory planning for a seasonal product

The online seller of a seasonal product faces fluctuations in demand throughout the year.

Without accurate forecasting, it is difficult to plan inventory, logistics, and marketing campaigns in advance.

This often leads to either excess stock or product shortages during peak seasons.

Therefore, the seller wants to develop a demand forecasting model to predict future sales and optimize purchasing, pricing, and marketing efforts.

Goal
An advanced, hybrid forecasting model

To build an advanced, hybrid forecasting model that accurately predicts future sales of a seasonal product by combining time series models, external data (e.g. reviews, weather, competitors), and language modeling (LLM-based topic modeling from customer reviews).

Data
Proprietary Wildberries (Russian E-commerce platform) UGG sales dataset
  • sales.csv: Daily product sales, price, discount (36 datasets)
  •  competitors.csv: Competitor sales, prices, and ratings
  •  reviews.csv: Customer comments and ratings
  •  weather.csv: Temperature, precipitation, weather conditions
  • All data is filtered for the city and spans multiple months or seasons. Input datasets: 36 monthly datasets (2022 to 2024)+ one dataset with comments of one ProductID.
Models Used
Time Series + LLM
  • Prophet for trend extraction
  • XGBoost for final supervised learning
  • SentenceTransformers for BERT-based embeddings
  • BERTopic for extracting dominant customer concerns
  • Lag, rolling average, calendar-based variables
Results
We used two evaluation metrics
  • RMSE(Root Mean Squared Error) - 4.15
  • MAE (Mean Absolute Error) - 2.38
  • Stockout rate ↓ 20%
Tools
Python libraries for data analysis, visualization, and model development.
  • Languages & Core Libraries: Python (pandas, numpy, datetime, os, glob, requests)
  • Data Analysis & Visualization: Matplotlib, Seaborn,Plotly
  • Feature Engineering & Preprocessing: Scikit-learn (LabelEncoder, train_test_split, GridSearchCV, MLPRegressor, MinMaxScaler, metrics ), TensorFlow / Keras(LSTM, GRU, CNN, Sequential, Adam, EarlyStopping), SciKeras (KerasRegressor), Holidays, Datetime
  • Machine Learning & Forecasting:Prophet, XGBoost (XGBRegressor)
Business Impact
Key outcomes demonstrating how the model improved decision-making.
  • Improved demand visibility for inventory and supply chain managers
  • Reduced wastage from overstocking
  • Anticipated seasonal peaks (e.g., holidays, snow)
  • Reacted faster to customer sentiment via topic trends
  • Better pricing and promotional strategies using competitor-aware forecasts
