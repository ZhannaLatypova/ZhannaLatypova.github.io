Analysis of product assortment for optimization

A comprehensive analysis of the product assortment helps identify sales inefficiencies and optimize the mix of products to increase profitability and customer satisfaction.

Client
The company operates in the e-commerce sector.

Selling consumer products online across multiple categories (electronics, fashion, home goods, etc.)..

Business Problem
Low-performing products were reducing profitability and occupying valuable warehouse and marketing resources.

The company had an extensive catalog of products, but not all items contributed equally to revenue and customer satisfaction.

Some products generated high traffic but poor conversion rates, while others were popular but underpromoted.

Without a structured analytical framework, the company lacked clarity on which products to prioritize, which to discount, and which to discontinue.

This led to inefficient inventory management and suboptimal marketing allocation.

Goal
To identify key performance drivers and optimize product portfolio decisions.
  • Discovering high-margin and fast-selling products.
  • Detecting low-performing SKUs for potential removal or repositioning.
  • Analyzing category-level trends and seasonality.
  • Providing recommendations for pricing, promotion, and stock optimization.
Data
The dataset contained product-level transactional.
  • Product ID, Category, Brand, and Price
  • Units Sold, Revenue, Profit Margin
  • Date of sale (for trend analysis)
  • Data Source: Internal sales and marketing database exported from the company’s CRM/e-commerce system.
  • Data Range: ~12 months of historical sales records.
Approach
This project used an exploratory and descriptive analytics approach, not predictive modeling.
  • Data Cleaning & Preparation: Handling missing values, normalizing prices, filtering anomalies.
  • Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA): Analyzing revenue distribution, profit margins, and customer preferences by category.
  • Segmentation / Classification: Using KPIs such as sales volume, profit margin, and return rate to group products.
Results
Two evaluation metrics
  • Found three underperforming categories with high return rates and low profitability.
  • Highlighted pricing inconsistencies causing margin erosion in two major categories.
Tools
Python libraries for analysis
  • Python (pandas, numpy, matplotlib, seaborn, plotly)
  • Scikit-learn (metrics)
  • Jupyter Notebook
  • Excel / Power BI for final dashboards
Business Impact
Better decision-making
  • Improved understanding of product profitability and lifecycle performance.
  • Data-driven reorganization of product catalog, removal of low-value SKUs and promotion of top-performing items.
  • Enabled smarter inventory planning and marketing strategy through product-level insights.
